Condition identified
Chronic Temporal Misalignment
The patient retains an internal sense of time that does not conform to external temporal demands. This manifests as resistance to scheduled waking, scheduled sleep, and scheduled productivity. The patient reports a desire to live at their own pace, which is incompatible with contemporary social and occupational requirements.

Left untreated, the patient's internal rhythm will continue to diverge from institutional time, resulting in reduced availability, missed deadlines, and negative evaluation by others.
Recommended course of action
A single medication is prescribed. It is designed to replace the patient's internal time with external time, allowing the patient to move, rest, and respond according to schedule — without internal resistance.

What this medication does

  • Suppresses your internal sense of time.
  • Allows you to act, rest, and focus according to the time required of you, rather than the time your body feels.
  • Removes the discomfort of being awake when you should sleep, or sleepy when you should work.
  • Reduces the awareness of time passing, so that hours of scheduled activity feel shorter, and personal time does not feel wasted.

Side effects

  • Gradual loss of the ability to feel small emotions during scheduled hours.
  • Difficulty forming long-term plans or a coherent sense of your own life.
  • A vague sense that time is no longer yours. This is expected and will stabilize.
